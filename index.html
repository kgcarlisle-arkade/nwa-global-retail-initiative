Every major retail and supply chain technology company in the world wants access to this market. The question is whether Northwest Arkansas captures that interest — or concedes it to more organized ecosystems.

The Challenge

The interest is here.
The infrastructure isn't.

"Without a formalized landing pathway, interest does not consistently translate into presence."
Today, without Land + Launch NWA:
  • International companies conduct cold outreach or visit Bentonville for 1–2 days of meetings — then leave
  • Introductions are made informally, without structure or sustained follow-through
  • No formalized pathway connects companies to workspace, pilot opportunities, regulatory navigation, or workforce
  • High-growth companies default to Austin, Atlanta, New York, and Toronto — ecosystems that actively receive them
  • Physical relocation, high-skill job creation, and long-term capital investment remain unrealized
The Solution

A structured platform.
A clear pathway.
A place to stay.

Land + Launch NWA is an annual cohort-based commercialization program supporting 6–12 growth-stage retail technology and supply chain companies through a structured six-month market entry engagement. Led by the Greater Bentonville Area Chamber of Commerce. Delivered by Arkade.

Participating companies receive dedicated commercialization support, curated introductions to retailers, suppliers, and investors, physical workspace at Arkade, regulatory and relocation navigation, and full integration into the regional innovation ecosystem.

The Partnership

Three organizations. Each essential. None sufficient alone.

Fiduciary & Civic Lead
Greater Bentonville Area Chamber of Commerce
Serves as the initiative's fiduciary, administering all grant funds and ensuring alignment with regional economic development priorities. The Chamber's Bold Bentonville strategic plan explicitly names this type of coordinated commercialization infrastructure as a priority — and names the Walton Family Foundation as a key partner in getting there. The Chamber has successfully deployed its 501(c)(3) status as fiduciary for prior regional innovation programs, providing a proven accountability structure for exactly this kind of public-private initiative.
Commercialization Delivery Partner
Arkade
Serves as the operating delivery partner under a formal agreement with the Chamber. Arkade executes the recruitment strategy, delivers all programming and support services, manages company relationships, tracks commercialization outcomes, and coordinates ecosystem engagement. Arkade brings validated experience converting global retail technology interest into pilots, partnerships, and sustained regional presence — doing what no other entity in NWA is currently structured to do.
On governance and accountability: All grant funds will be administered through the Chamber, allocated strictly toward approved programmatic activities, and reported against clear KPIs on a quarterly basis. Annual public economic impact reporting will be produced. The initiative is designed as catalytic funding with a built-in sustainability path — not a permanent operating subsidy.
