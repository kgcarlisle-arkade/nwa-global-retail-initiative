Section I

Executive Summary

02

Northwest Arkansas is home to one of the most concentrated industry ecosystems in the world.

Five global headquarters anchor the region's core industries — consumer commerce (Walmart, Sam's Club), food and agriculture (Tyson Foods, Simmons Foods), and logistics and transportation (J.B. Hunt) — alongside more than 1,100 CPG brand offices. Three of those companies — Walmart, Tyson Foods, and J.B. Hunt — rank in the Fortune 500. Combined annual revenue from NWA-headquartered companies exceeds $750 billion, more than the GDP of metropolitan Boston.

While the region continues to grow in economic strength and national visibility, it lacks the structured infrastructure to intentionally convert global founder interest into measurable regional economic impact. Out-of-market growth-stage B2B technology companies want access to Bentonville's industry leaders and ecosystem. However, without a formalized landing pathway, curated programming, and coordinated regional infrastructure, interest does not consistently translate into sustained presence, ecosystem participation, or long-term contribution to the region.

The Greater Bentonville Area Chamber of Commerce, in partnership with Arkade, proposes a three-year initiative to build the structured ecosystem infrastructure NWA needs — supporting growth-stage B2B technology companies serving the region's anchor industries through cohort-based programming, ecosystem integration, and community-anchored support. Land + Launch NWA will be administered by the Chamber as fiduciary partner and delivered by Arkade's commercial growth platform.

Land + Launch NWA is built around the founders themselves — providing the workspace, programming, mentorship, and ecosystem access growth-stage companies need to commit to Northwest Arkansas long-term.

This effort will:

  • Engage growth-stage B2B technology companies serving consumer commerce, food and agriculture, and logistics and transportation
  • Connect global founders to NWA's ecosystem — mentors, advisors, talent, and industry leaders
  • Strengthen regional innovation programming open to cohort participants and the broader community
  • Elevate Bentonville's national and global profile as a hub for applied innovation
  • Build structured pathways between local talent and the next generation of high-growth companies

We are seeking $1.5 million in catalytic grant funding, matched by approximately $822,000 in private and in-kind contributions from Arkade and the Chamber over three years. Grant funding will support five key components: Ecosystem Intelligence and Strategic Positioning; Program Recruitment and Founder Curation; Innovation Programming and Industry Convenings; Embedding and Expansion Support; and Talent and Ecosystem Integration.

Section II

The Challenge: Opportunity without Infrastructure

03

Northwest Arkansas offers a rare concentration of global industry leadership across consumer commerce, food and agriculture, and logistics and transportation, alongside major CPG presence, a sophisticated talent pool, rapid population growth, and supportive economic development leadership. However, the region competes globally for innovation companies that often default to coastal U.S. cities or other international hubs. The region currently lacks structured ecosystem infrastructure to intentionally connect global founders, talent, and industry.

At the same time, international and out-of-market growth-stage B2B technology companies face significant barriers when entering U.S. enterprise markets, including limited access to decision-makers, regulatory and operational complexity, lack of trusted local networks, high cost of entry, and cultural and commercial differences.

Today, without Land + Launch NWA:

  • Companies from outside the region conduct cold outreach or visit Bentonville for 1–2 days of meetings
  • Introductions are made informally, without structure or sustained follow-through
  • Most companies leave without establishing a sustained presence
  • There is no formalized pathway connecting founders to workspace, programming, mentorship, regulatory navigation, workforce, and ecosystem support

Without intentional ecosystem infrastructure connecting founders to industry leaders, programming, workspace, regulatory navigation, workforce pipelines, and economic development support, Northwest Arkansas risks losing high-growth companies to more coordinated ecosystems such as Austin, Atlanta, New York, and Toronto.

The intent is to bridge those barriers with structured programming, ecosystem integration, and community-anchored support — positioning Northwest Arkansas as a leading U.S. hub for applied innovation in consumer commerce, food, and logistics.

Section III

The Solution: Land + Launch NWA

04

Program Overview

The partnership will launch Land + Launch NWA — a structured, cohort-based, and community-integrated initiative supporting growth-stage B2B technology companies serving Northwest Arkansas' anchor industries: consumer commerce, food and agriculture, and logistics and transportation.

This initiative establishes a clear and supported pathway for international and out-of-market founders to engage with the region, build relationships across industry, talent, and capital, and integrate into the broader NWA innovation ecosystem.

Land + Launch NWA will operate as an annual cohort-based initiative supporting 6–12 growth-stage technology companies through a structured six-month engagement. Participating companies will receive dedicated guidance, curated introductions to enterprise partners, investors, and ecosystem leaders, access to physical workspace at Arkade, and business establishment support through Chamber resources.

The program provides structured market immersion, regulatory and relocation navigation, industry and cultural education, ongoing matchmaking, and full integration into the broader regional innovation ecosystem, ensuring companies are positioned to build partnerships, hire locally, and establish long-term presence in Northwest Arkansas.

Land + Launch NWA is built around the founders themselves — providing the workspace, programming, mentorship, and ecosystem access that growth-stage companies need to commit to Northwest Arkansas long-term.

Target Company Profile

  • Native to an international (primary) or a non-local U.S. (secondary) market
  • B2B technology serving NWA's anchor industries — including consumer commerce (in-store, ecommerce, omnichannel), food and agriculture, logistics and transportation, AI and data analytics, automation and robotics, and sustainability and packaging
  • Series A+ growth-stage startups
  • Demonstrated product-market fit, revenue generating or late-stage pilot phase
  • Companies actively seeking U.S. market entrance or expansion
  • Firms capable of hiring locally within 12 months

Section IX

The Proven Model: Regional Comparables

11

The structured ecosystem infrastructure this initiative proposes is not an untested concept — it is an established and growing category of regional investment that has produced measurable community and economic returns in comparable metros across the U.S. and internationally. NWA is not pioneering a new idea. It is building on a proven one.

Opportunity Austin / Global Launch Pad (Austin, TX)

Backed by the Austin Chamber of Commerce — structurally the most direct analog to what Arkade and the Chamber are proposing — Austin's program has contributed to 400+ international companies now operating in the region. Austin is the most direct U.S. market competitor to NWA for attracting growth-stage technology companies. The fact that Austin has formalized a Chamber-backed ecosystem program while NWA has not is a competitive risk this initiative directly addresses.

Georgia Tech Enterprise Innovation Institute (Atlanta, GA)

Since launching in 2018, Georgia Tech's cohort-based program has achieved a 68% U.S. expansion rate — 15 of 22 participating international companies successfully expanded into the U.S. market. This compares to an industry average of approximately 30% for informal market entry, demonstrating that structured programming — not just introductions — is the decisive variable in conversion.

Toronto Global (Toronto, Ontario)

The closest operational model to what Arkade and the Chamber are proposing, at a larger scale. Over its history, Toronto Global has facilitated 260 structured engagements generating 33,000+ jobs and $1.3 billion CAD in cumulative community tax impact. In 2023/24 alone, 44 structured engagements produced 5,527 jobs and $217.5M CAD in tax impact. These numbers establish that structured ecosystem investment programs are high-return regional infrastructure.

The bottom line: Every comparable metro that has built this infrastructure has seen measurable community and economic returns. NWA has everything those metros have — and in consumer commerce, food, and logistics, considerably more. What it has lacked is the infrastructure to connect that advantage to opportunity. This initiative builds it.

Sections X & XI

The Partnership Case & Long-Term Vision

12

The Partnership Case

Northwest Arkansas sits at a rare and time-sensitive inflection point. It has global industry concentration, a growing talent base, civic momentum, and the institutional density that most regions spend decades trying to build. What it lacks — in the Chamber's own words — is "a more coordinated effort among all entities" and "a dedicated facility for innovation startups." This initiative is the direct answer to that gap.

Two organizations — each essential, neither sufficient alone — are positioned to close it.

Fiduciary & Civic Lead

Greater Bentonville Area Chamber of Commerce

Brings the civic mandate and institutional credibility to make this work at a regional scale. Its own Bold Bentonville strategic plan explicitly names this kind of coordinated ecosystem infrastructure as a priority — with a proven 501(c)(3) fiduciary structure for public-private initiatives. The Chamber does not just endorse this initiative. It has been calling for it.

Program Delivery Partner

Arkade

Brings the platform and commercial expertise to deliver what the ecosystem has been missing. Designed to help businesses from outside the region gain access to NWA's innovation ecosystem, Arkade connects founders to the mentors, advisors, industry leaders, and partners they need to succeed. Arkade does not replicate what exists — it delivers what no other entity in the region is currently structured to do.

The initiative is supported by a coalition of senior operators, investors, and industry leaders committed to strengthening Northwest Arkansas as a hub for consumer commerce, food, and logistics innovation. Together, this partnership structure delivers the full stack that regional ecosystem development requires — private-sector execution and civic accountability — building something Northwest Arkansas will benefit from for decades.

Long-Term Vision

Over time, the program will evolve into a self-sustaining, self-reinforcing platform supported by corporate sponsorships, membership participation, private investment partnerships, and revenue-generating services. This structure ensures enduring impact while scaling access and opportunities for global founders building across consumer commerce, food, and logistics.

Within five years, Northwest Arkansas will be recognized as a leading U.S. hub for applied innovation in consumer commerce, food, and logistics — the place where global founders, talent, and industry connect at scale. The region will become a magnet for high-growth companies, cultivate a nationally recognized innovation ecosystem, and provide a lasting home for international firms building U.S. operations.

Through strong public-private collaboration, this initiative will catalyze innovation, accelerate job creation, and drive sustained ecosystem growth, solidifying Northwest Arkansas' position as a globally competitive, innovation-driven region.
